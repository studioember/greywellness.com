## Sleep & Insomnia

Insomnia isn't just about having trouble falling asleep. It can look like waking up in the middle of the night and not being able to get back to sleep, rising too early, or never feeling truly rested no matter how many hours you log. Over time, poor sleep affects your concentration, your mood, your immune system, and your ability to cope with everyday stress. 

There is a way forward. Together, we work to identify the thoughts, habits, and patterns keeping you awake and replace them with ones that actually support rest. The approach is evidence-based and personalized to you, and many people see meaningful improvement without relying on medication long-term.

<a id="trauma"></a>
## Trauma

Trauma is the lasting impact of experiences that overwhelmed your ability to cope, whether that's a single event or something that happened repeatedly over time. It can show up as flashbacks, emotional numbness, difficulty trusting others, feeling constantly on edge, or reacting to present situations in ways that feel disproportionate. Trauma isn't a sign of weakness; it's the nervous system doing its best to protect you.

Healing is possible. We work at a pace that feels safe for you, helping your nervous system gradually release what it has been holding. Over time, the weight of the past becomes lighter, and you begin to feel more present, grounded, and like yourself again.

<a id="childhood-adverse-experiences"></a>
## Childhood Adverse Experiences

Adverse childhood experiences, things like neglect, abuse, household instability, or loss, don't stay in the past. They shape the way the brain and body develop, and often show up in adulthood as difficulties with emotional regulation, self-worth, relationships, and physical health. Many people don't connect their current struggles to what happened early in life, but the link is real and well-documented.

Understanding where these patterns come from is the first step to changing them. Together, we work to help you make sense of your story, reconnect with your body, and build the foundation for a life that feels stable, safe, and genuinely yours.

<a id="stress-management"></a>
## Stress Management

Stress is the body's natural response to demands that feel bigger than your current resources. But when it becomes constant, it stops being useful and starts being harmful. Chronic stress can show up as physical tension, fatigue, irritability, difficulty sleeping, or simply feeling like you are always running on empty with no real chance to recover.

It doesn't have to stay that way. Together, we work to understand what is driving your stress, how it is showing up in your body and mind, and what practical tools can help you build real resilience. Over time, you start to feel less reactive, more grounded, and better equipped to handle whatever life brings.
